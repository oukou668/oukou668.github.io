This work studies large language model evaluation as a measurement problem rather than a simple averaging problem. It develops an interpretable multidimensional item-response framework for sparse model-by-benchmark score matrices, with explicit representations of model capability, benchmark difficulty, and benchmark discrimination.

The project also produces auditable artifacts for benchmark validity, taxonomy alignment, residual anomalies, ranking stability, and compact benchmark-suite design.
