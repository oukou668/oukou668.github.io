I am a postgraduate student in Computer Science at **Fudan University**. My research focuses on making large language model evaluation more interpretable, reliable, and useful.

Research Interests
======

- **LLM evaluation and measurement science:** moving beyond flat leaderboard averages toward models of capability, benchmark difficulty, and discrimination.
- **Agent evaluation**: studying how benchmarks measure agent capabilities such as planning, tool use, memory, and adaptation, with an emphasis on validity, robustness, and reproducibility.
- **On-device language**： models: efficient LLM inference and evaluation on resource-constrained devices.

Current Research
======

My current research develops a **Meta-evaluation framework for model and benchmarks**. Using multidimensional item response theory and an interpretable tag bridge, I estimate model capabilities, benchmark difficulty, and the specific abilities that each benchmark primarily assesses. The goal is to produce stable, auditable summaries of model capabilities and benchmark demands, emphasizing reproducible capability tiers rather than brittle exact rankings.

Projects
======

- [**Meta Evaluation Dashboard**](https://oukou668.github.io/nhjkgyuf/) — an interactive dashboard for exploring model–benchmark structure, capability profiles, and evaluation evidence.
- [**Xiaomi 17 LLM Benchmark**](https://github.com/oukou668/edgellm-xiaomi) — an Android benchmark scaffold for evaluating small language models with exportable accuracy, memory, battery, and thermal reports.
